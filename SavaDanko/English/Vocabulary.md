**comprise** - to have somebody/something as parts or members

---
**accord** - a formal agreement between two organizations, countries, etc.
- The two sides signed a peace accord last July.

---
**affect somebody/something** - to produce a change in somebody/something
- How will these changes affect us?
- The article deals with issues affecting the lives of children.

---
**involve** - вовлекать
- *involve something* -  Any investment involves an element of risk.
- *involve doing something* - The test will involve answering questions about a photograph.
- *involve somebody/something doing something* - The job involves me travelling all over the country.

---
**transmission** - the act or process of passing something from one person, place or thing to another
- the transmission of the disease
**transmission** - the act or process of sending out an electronic signal or message or of broadcasting a radio or television programme
- the transmission of computer data along telephone lines

---
**perform** - выполнять
- The engine seems to be performing well.
- The company has been performing poorly over the past year.

---
**this is nothing but** - это не что иное, как

---
**rudimentary**(basic) - dealing with only the most basic matters or ideas
- His understanding of the language is very rudimentary

---
**distinguish** - to recognize the difference between two people or things
- distinguish between A and B
- At what age are children able to distinguish between right and wrong?

---
**propose** - to suggest a plan, an idea, etc. for people to think about and decide on
- The government proposed changes to the voting system.

---
**prodigy** - a young person whose intelligence or skill is unusually good for their age

---
**marvel** - ​a wonderful and surprising person or thing

---
**consume** - потреблять

---
**supply** - производить

---
**facilitate** - facilitate something to make an action or a process possible or easier
- The new trade agreement should facilitate more rapid economic growth.

---
**explicit** - clear and easy to understand, so that you have no doubt what is meant (явно)

---
**interference** - the act of getting involved in and trying to influence a situation that should not really involve you, in a way that annoys other people

---
**commence** - to begin to happen; to begin something
- The meeting is scheduled to commence at noon.
- I will be on leave during the week commencing 15 February.

---
**indiscreet** - not careful about what you say or do, especially when you reveal something secret or something that could be embarrassing or offensive
- One indiscreet comment could spoil the whole thing.
- It was indiscreet of him to disclose that information.

---
**curious** - ​having a strong desire to know about something
- He is such a curious boy, always asking questions.
- They were very curious about the people who lived upstairs.
